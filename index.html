Artista

Status: Complete

Time frame: Fall 2022 (8 weeks)

Team Size: 4

Artista is a grid-based competetive adventure board game with a large emphasis on violence and creulty. Players race to compete their masterpiece and become the Artista, the most powerful and cutthroat creative in the city of Chiaroscuro. To get to this point however, they have to hold a tight grasp on their sanity and find inspiration to get ahead of their rivals.

This game was created in a small team of 4 people as part of a game design course. As the only Game Design and Development major in the group, I ended up designing a large portion of the gameplay loop and gave feedback on classes and mechanics other team members designed for the experience. I also played a large part in the organization and proofreading of our documents and rules, which helped to keep the group largely organized and focused on the content of the game.

Evergreen Escapades

Status: On Hiatus

Time frame: Summer - Fall 2022 (4 months)

Engine Used: MonoGame (C#)

Tools Used: Visual Studios, Tiled

Evergreen Escapades side-view action platformer, where the player battles enemies while traveling from town to town, earning money to upgrade their equipment and train new abilities. Time is always moving forward, changing the threats you face as day transitions to night.

Being built in MonoGame, most elements of the game are made from scratch, as there is little support native to MonoGame outside of tracking time and uploading sprites. I programmed in my own system for running animations and collisions so that the game could function as intended, something that became leagues more complicated when I made the decision to have slopes. I also created a system for parallaxing the background infinitely, which ended up being much harder than expected. The environment was designed in Tiled, which is an open source program for building levels with tilesets. All levels are imported to MonoGame through text files, which I read in to decide what tiles are placed in the world and the properties of each tile.

This project went on hold when as my life became more busy and there wasn't time to make substantial progress on it. The main problem is that creating stages in tiled takes a massive amount of time for 1 person. When I return to this project, my first steps will be working on procedural level generation to (hopefully) speed up the rate at which I can work on this game.

Confrontation

Status: Ongoing

Time frame: Winter 2023 (2 months)

Engine Used: Unity (C#)

Tools Used: Jetbrains Rider

Confrontation is gearing up to be a story of galactic conquest against your enemies. The player takes control of planets and wages intergalactic battle to take over the universe, one solar system at a time. Quick thinking and solid tactics are expected from a great leader, and they'll be required in order to gain the upper hand against enemies.

Confrontation is a real-time strategy game based around resource and territory managment. The player will take control of planets and generate income from each planet they control, which can in turn be spent to increase troop production or capacity on each of their planets. All this will need to be done while waging war against AI, who will be doing the same thing right alongside you.

This project started out as a class coding project but quickly turned into a personal one. The main goals were the programming of autonomous agents and pathfinding. State machines are used to control ship behavior, with each state having it's own unique set of behaviors relateed to ally and enemy ships to make them orbit, travel, and battle in realistic ways. Progress has been good so far and I hope to begin making levels once enemy's begin to behave on their own.

Spaceway Skirmish

Status: Complete

Time frame: Fall 2022 (~4 weeks)

Engine Used: Unity (C#)

Tools Used: Jetbrains Rider

Spaceway Skirmish sees you fighting of the front lines of a stellar conflict. Seeking only glory, you venture out to make scrap metal of all ships that would dare oppose you. Whether it be enemy ships or the kepler belt, you won't stop fighting until someone takes you down for good.

Spaceway Skirmish class project whos primary goal was the introduction to the Unity Engine. The goal was to make a shoot-em-up with a score system. The only real requirenments were to have enemies and be able to fight them, so I wen't pretty far beyond the goal to involve parallaxing and animations, which let me gain a general understanding of the sprite editor and workflow. The most complex thing I did was create a system so that no enemies would overlap, which involved writing a sorting algorithm to put them in order so that it could quickly be identified where open space was. All things considered it is a relatively small project but it helped build a greater understanding for how to program with components, as compared to the hierarchical nightmare I was used to with MonoGame.
